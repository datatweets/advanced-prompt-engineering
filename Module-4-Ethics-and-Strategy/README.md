# Module 4: Ethics and Strategy

![Ethics](https://img.shields.io/badge/focus-responsible%20AI-7c3aed)
![Strategy](https://img.shields.io/badge/output-guidelines%20and%20workflow-16a34a)

This module explains how to use AI responsibly and strategically. It covers privacy, bias, hallucinations, intellectual property, content distribution, team guidelines, and quality control.

## Duration

Total module time: 150 minutes.

| Lesson | Time | Teaching focus |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| [01-responsible-ai-use.md](01-responsible-ai-use.md) | 50 min | Hallucinations, privacy, bias, human review, risk spectrum, and review checklists |
| [02-content-distribution.md](02-content-distribution.md) | 50 min | Goal-first planning, audience journey, channel adaptation, cadence, and measurement |
| [03-building-guidelines.md](03-building-guidelines.md) | 50 min | Team AI guidelines, prompt standards, escalation rules, and practical team rollout |

## Learning Outcomes

After this module, learners should be able to:

1. Identify common AI risks and apply the appropriate review level for each.
2. Build a safer drafting prompt that separates facts from inferences.
3. Plan content distribution by goal, audience journey stage, and channel.
4. Write practical one-page team guidelines with approved uses, restrictions, and escalation rules.
5. Apply a five-step team rollout to introduce AI workflows to colleagues.

## Module Scenario

A corporate communications team wants to use AI for research summaries, campaign drafts, and internal memos. The organization needs a process that improves productivity without exposing private data or publishing unreliable content.

## Governance Rule

If AI output affects customers, employees, money, reputation, or compliance, add human review.
